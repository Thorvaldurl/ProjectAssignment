In a high-school game of "Killer" in which a student must shoot another with a squirt gun, Matt Arnold (Ben Foster) has to "kill" classmate Jenny Herk (Zooey Deschanel), and decides to sneak up on her at home. By coincidence, 2 hit-men Henry (Dennis Farina) and Leonard (Jack Kehler) are also there to kill Arthur Herk (Stanley Tucci), who has secretly embezzled money from his company, Penultra Corp. Henry and Leonard had flown in that morning from New Orleans. When the fake assassination attempt crosses paths with the real one, police officers Monica Romero (Janeane Garofalo) and Walter Kramitz (Patrick Warburton) are called out to the resulting disturbance. During the chaos of the assassination attempts, Matt's friend Andrew (DJ Qualls) calls Eliot Arnold (Tim Allen), Matt's father. Eliot is a divorced man, fired from his job at the Miami Herald and runs a struggling advertising agency. Matt thinks he is a loser. Eliot's clients are unhappy with him as he comes up with creative advertisements, but clients want girls in bikinis to be selling stuff on posters. Before this Eliot wrote an offbeat column for the Miami Herald for 18 years. He had won 2 Pulitzer prizes for his work. He is fired when a new guy becomes his boss and Eliot ignores his orders for completing specific assignments, over his own passion projects. Earlier the same day he had found that his wife was cheating on him with the tennis instructor.
